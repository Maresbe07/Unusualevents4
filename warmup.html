1. Has John ever been to Italy?

Answers

A. Yes, he has

B. No, he hasn't 

Feedback

Great! 

The answer is grammatically correct because the use of the auxiliary verb "has" agrees with the subject "he" and it is the short answer to the present perfect tense.

On the other side, it is the right answer because his answer was positive. "Yes, he has" is a positive answer for a third person (he, she, it)

Moreover, grammatically, to respond to short answers with the present tense in negative form, the answers must use "subject, in this case, “he" + “the auxiliary verb “has” if it is for a third person” + “the negative word "not"

Multi-choice

Question

3. Has John ever been to Ireland?

Answers

A. No, he hasn’t

B. No, I haven’t

Feedback

Fantastic!

Grammatically, “A” is the correct answer because the subject “he” belongs to a third person (he, she, it), and you must use the auxiliary verb “has” + the negative word “not” in the present perfect tense.

On the other hand, the answer is correct because he answered negatively. So, that is the correct answer for a third-person in a negative form. "No, he hasn't"

Try again.

This answer is incorrect because you are answering questions in the third person. The subject “I” and the auxiliary verb “have” do not belong to a third person.  The auxiliary verb “have” works with other subjects, e.g.

I have

You have

We have

You have  

They have

Solution

  1. Correct Option (Feedback)
  2. Wrong (Feedback)

Question

4. Has he ever played golf?

Answers

A. Yes, he has played golf.

B. No, he has never played golf.

Feedback

Try again. 

This answer is incorrect because he gave a negative answer, and this is the correct way to make long positive answers in the present perfect tense.

Fantastic!

The answer is grammatically correct because the use of the auxiliary verb "has" agrees with the subject "he" and it is a long negative answer used in the present perfect tense. The past participle "played" is used to form the present perfect tense.

On the other side, it is the right answer because his answer was negative. So, "never" is another word to answer negatively.
